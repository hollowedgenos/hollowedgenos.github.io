# Baby's First Game
{: .text-center}
---

The first console I ever owned was a Gameboy Advanced SP. It provided me with hours of enjoyment in my early childhood. Throughout the time I owned that console I had played many games, but the first game I ever played, is the one that started it all. Super Mario Brothers 3 will always be a game I consider a classic, its simple but engaging gameplay kept me utterly enthralled. You go from one level to the next, jumping on platforms, avoiding, or stomping on enemies, collecting coins and power-ups along the way. I remember countless times of having to start the game over again after losing all my lives, but I never wanted to quit. The game offered challenges and it was rewarding to overcome those challenges. That is what most definitely got my friends hooked. Some of my fondest memories of Super Mario Brothers 3 came from bonding with friends over it. We always tried to outdo each other by getting a higher score or to a level no one else reached before. These experiences are the foundation of what inspired me to pursue a career in the game industry.

# Hopes and Dreams
{: .text-center}

Independent Developers have always been a huge inspiration to me. The ever expansive worlds they come up with always leave me in awe. It doesn't come at much of a surprise I want to work at an indie dev studio, as a game designer or programmer. Indie devs are usually the people that take risks when making games. They toy and experiment with new ideas that most triple A publishers would be hesitant to support. The creative freedom indie devs have is what keeps this industry moving forward as they push the boundaries of what games can be. A few games that inspire me, games like OMORI, Yume Nikki, Undertale, Deltrarune, LISA: The Painful, Disco Elysium, and ULTRAKILL are all made by indie devs. Each game I have mentioned brings something fresh to the industry, either through intuitive and engaging gameplay or its gripping narrative. To work at an indie studio would mean to explore new avenues when it comes to developing games.

# The Importance of Video Games
{: .text-center}

While there are those that would call video games a waste of time, that could not be further from the truth. Video games are experiences, they are interactive stories with rich narratives. These narratives can make a genuine emotional impact on the player and can change their perspective of the world around them. They challenge the player to be better than they already are. Video games offer the player a chance to grow by challenging critical thinking and problem-solving skills which translate into everyday living. Games also help form friendships and build a sense of community. Some of my long-time friends I met were through online games. 

Video games are an artform. Their interactive nature allows them to convey complex thoughts and feelings to the player in a way that does not compare to reading literature, watching a movie, or observing a painting. To build this industry is to foster human expression, it is to innovate and explore new ideas, it is to connect with oneself and others, it is to be human.
