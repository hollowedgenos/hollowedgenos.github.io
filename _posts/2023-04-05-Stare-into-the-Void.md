Post Void is an enigmatic indie shooter that is an absolute overload for the senses. Developed and published by independent company YCJY Games, Post Void was released on Steam on the 6th of August 2020. Before I get into the gamer further, I need to put a disclaimer here. Post Void is a game that features many flashing lights and imagery which may not be suitable for those who are photosensitive. With that out of the way, let us stare into the void together.

---

# A State of Trance Overwhelms the Senses
{: .text-center}

![A player landing a well-placed headshot.]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/post-void-gameplay_1-1.jpg){: .align-center}
*Getting headshots is extremely satisfying in this game.*
{: .text-center .small}

Pardon me, however, any formal structure of writing from now on is going to be thrown out of this blog post’s metaphorical window. Post Void is a fast-paced shooter which takes artistic inspiration from the older “boomer shooter” era of games (I.e., Wolfenstein, DOOM). Post Void is a game that seems to defy any logical reasoning or explanation; it just is, it exists as an element of our current universe and embraces unadulterated chaos.

![Sprinting through unrecognizable hallways.]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/post-void-gameplay_1-2.jpg){: .align-center}
*Oversaturated madness painted on your screen.*
{: .text-center .small}

Flashing lights pollute the screen upon launch, imagery a viewer may recognize only to forget the next moment may blip for a split second. Sensory overload is the mother of which the player is born from in Post Void and continues to be nurtured from. Firearms make the screen flash when fired, enemies can spontaneously combust into flames, headshots result in stray gore sticking to the walls and ceiling, bullets fired by enemies move quickly as flashing sprites across the screen. Every aspect of this game jumps out at the player with the subtlety of a semi driving into on-coming traffic. Even the main theme song of the game fully embraces its own chaos. Guitars scream a melody of annihilation in the player’s ears as they mow down enemy upon enemy. The developers knew exactly what they were doing when making this game and it is pure art. 

---

# Psychedelic, Surreal Gameplay
{: .text-center}

![The ending to a level, The Pool.]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/post-void-gameplay_1-3.jpg){: .align-center}
*The Pool awaits you at the end of the carnage.*
{: .text-center .small}

The gameplay is fairly simple in Post Void as you are able to walk, jump, slide and shoot your gun. Using these basic controls, the player must navigate a level which presents itself as a labyrinth of rooms, these rooms having enemies that can be dispatched. The goal is to get to the end of the labyrinth where a pool of glowing liquid waits for the player.

This is the transition point from one level to the next and the player can choose from one of three upgrades presented to them at the end of the level. Some upgrades allow for faster reloading of weapons and some give the player an entirely new weapon (which the player can only have one specific weapon in a run). As the player reaches new levels, the layout of the labyrinth will change, as well as diversifying the enemies that must be dealt with. Now this seems all standard for a very basic first-person shooter. What sets Post Void apart is how it incentivizes the player to keep moving and to keep killing enemies.

![The upgrade screen.]({{ site.url }}{{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/post-void-gameplay_1-4.jpg){: .align-center}
*The Pool allows you to upgrade yourself before beginning a new level.*
{: .text-center .small}

Besides a pistol, the player always carries a severed head with them. This head constantly leaks a bright white fluid from the neck area. This fluid is the players health and the only way to refill the head is to kill enemies. Failure to do so will result in the player dying. The death screen assaults the player with a barrage of symbols and flashing lights before prompting them to restart. The player must start from the first level again if they die during a run.

---

# Could Use More Off Switches
{: .text-center}

However, I do believe Post Void would benefit from more extensive accessibility settings. Currently there is a setting that gets rid of the flashing lights, but it is reduced to a simple toggle. The option for the player to specifically edit what effects they want in the game should be a feature. I realize there is the question of a works’ artistic integrity being compromised, but there are ways to find a middle ground when it comes to accessibility and the vision of the developers. 

---

# Stare Into The Void, It Stares Back
{: .text-center}

The imperative of Post Void is presented through its own existence. One and zeroes compiled to create an experience which fuels that fight response in the human brain. Move fast, keep killing, stay alive. While simple and hectic, it is extremely engaging and I definitely recommend it!
