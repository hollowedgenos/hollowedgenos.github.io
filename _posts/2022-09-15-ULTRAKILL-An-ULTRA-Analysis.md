Recently I played ULTRAKILL, an FPS that takes heavy inspiration from games like Quake and Devil May Cry. This game is an absolute trip.


<p>Published by New Blood Interactive and developed by indie dev, Arsi “Hakita” Patala, ULTRAKILL was released on September 3, 2020, under Early Access on Steam. Nearly a year and a half later I never really gave it much mind. Until a friend gifted me the game over Steam and now I’m punching myself thousands of times wondering why I didn’t play it sooner. ULTRAKILL is a fast-paced movement shooter that pits you against droves of demons from hell. Each level has a series of interconnected rooms, each room the player enters must be cleared of all enemies to progress. So that’s the core gameplay loop? Go in a room, shoot all the enemies, and leave? Yes and no.</p>

---

# Fast, Rewarding, and Fun
{: .text-center}

ULTRAKILL’s gameplay strives to challenge and through this challenge the player becomes more competent as they progress. ULTRAKILL encourages the player to experiment with the movement and weapon system and how they interact with one another. Further experimentation is incentivized by a scoring system called the style meter.

The style meter goes up in real-time when the player is chaining weapon combinations together and avoiding damage through constant movement. Through this, the game organically reinforces a more aggressive playstyle. Believe me, you need to be aggressive in this game. Enemies will constantly chase and/or shoot at you and you have to be proactive with prioritizing targets, switching weapons, and utilizing the movement system to its fullest. This all may sound overwhelming, but ULTRAKILL makes learning these mechanics extremely rewarding, which is an accomplishment in itself in the context of game design.

# <span style="display: block; text-align: center">“Mankind is Dead,<br>Blood is Fuel,<br>Hell is Full"</span>

These are the first words that pop up on your screen when you first start ULTRAKILL’s campaign. 

For context, ULTRAKILL takes place in a future where all humans are extinct, and the only remnants left of humanity are the robots they created specifically for war. These robots do not use electricity but use blood as fuel. Because humanity is extinct, the robots need a new source of blood to keep themselves alive. It’s not stated how, but at some point, the robots found a way to get into Hell and now demon blood is on the menu. 

This is where your character comes in. You play as a robot named ‘V1’—whose motivations are unknown—who is venturing through the many layers of Hell, slaying robots and demons that stand in its way. The game is currently split into two acts, the first act follows V1 as they fight through Hells layers of Limbo, Lust, and Gluttony. Act two was released as of August of this year and has V1 fiercely blasting their way through the layers of Greed, Wrath, and Heresy. Because this game is still in early access on Steam, Act three is not yet released as it is still in development.

# Nearly Perfect
{: .text-center}

ULTRAKILL does a lot right, it does have a few issues however. There are a few arenas in this game that feel very bare bones. Specifically, level 5-2, this level has V1 walking on rickety old docks in a never-ending sea of damned souls. However, the first half of this level feels like there could be more added to it. You are extremely limited in where you can move when walking on the docks as you usually are moving forward. Previous levels allowed for a lot more room to move and playing this level felt underwhelming. I think it is best to keep the player unrestricted in their movement through the level and keeping things from looking too linear.

# Play This Game Now
{: .text-center}

ULTRAKILL is a blast of a game. I absolutely love the weapon and movement systems. The way they synergize with one another is nothing short of a stroke of genius. The feeling you get from mastering these mechanics is like no other. The style system is a great aspect of the gameplay as well, getting feedback on how good you are doing is a great motivator to get the player really invested in the game. There’s so much more I could talk about but then this blog post would go on forever. I heavily recommend ULTRAKILL as it’s really the best the movement-shooter genre has to offer as of recent. 
This game is awesome in all of its blood, guts, and glory, so go play it!
